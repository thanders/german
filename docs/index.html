German Cases

German grammar has four cases. The cases explain which function the noun has and how it relates to the other words in the sentence.

Overview
Case When to use it? Example Question
Nominative The nominative case describes the subject (the person or thing that performs the action). . Das Pferd ist weiß Wer or Was (who or what)?
Akkusativ is known as the direct object. It is the thing that is acted upon (it „receives“ the action) Der Junge schenkt einer Freundin die Blumen. Wen oder was? (Whom or what)?
Dativ is used to show the is known as the indirect object. The indirect object is normally the noun that receives something (normally the direct object, which is in the accusative case). Er hilft dem Mann beim Putzen Wem oder was (whom or who)?
Genetiv shows possession or belonging Das Pferd des Bauers ist weiß. Whose...?
